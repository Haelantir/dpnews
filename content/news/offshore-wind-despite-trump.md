Offshore wind farms are actively taking shape off Rhode Island's coast. Revolution Wind is over 90% complete and already delivering power to New England's grid, while South Fork Wind is in its second year of commercial operation. / Despite this progress, the Trump administration has halted leasing and permitting, paused active construction and arranged a $1 billion payout for a French company to exit U.S. offshore wind development. Federal judges have struck down some of these orders. / Industry advocates warn that blocking offshore wind drives up utility bills as electricity demand surges and coastal states have few large-scale alternatives. Vineyard Wind recently finished construction, with Empire Wind and Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind still underway.
